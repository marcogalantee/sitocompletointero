Push-Up

how to do it:

Start in a plank position with hands aligned under your shoulders, legs straight, and body in a straight line. Lower your chest until it nearly touches the floor, then push back up to return to the starting position.

What is it for:

Strengthens and stabilizes the upper body, increasing arm and shoulder endurance and improving body control during pushing movements.

Diamond Push-Up

how to do it:

Similar to a traditional push-up, but place your hands together under your chest, forming a diamond shape. Lower your chest toward your hands, then push up.

What is it for:

Increases arm strength, focusing on the inner arm area to improve stability in movements requiring precision.

Plyometric (Explosive) Push-Up

how to do it:

Perform a regular push-up, but push off forcefully to lift your hands off the floor, landing with control.

What is it for:

Enhances explosiveness and speed, preparing you for quick movements that require coordination and strength.

Chair Dips

how to do it:

Place two chairs beside your hips and rest your hands on them. Lower yourself until your elbows form a 90-degree angle, then push back up.

What is it for:

Increases endurance in downward pressing movements, improving your ability to support your body weight.

Pike Push-Up

how to do it:

Begin in a downward-facing dog position (as in yoga), lower your head toward the floor by bending your elbows, then push back up.

What is it for:

Strengthens the shoulders and prepares the body for vertical pressing movements, helpful for stabilizing the upper body in upward movements.
